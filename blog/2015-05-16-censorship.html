On censorship and our recent absence

Update, 28 February 2017: We now use thumbnails to get around this issue, and Vimeo is no longer the default video player.

The front page is much uglier: every video is replaced by that placeholder image, which informs you that Vimeo is blocked because of (amongst other things) pornographic content. It’s unfortunate that innocuous videos about plants and ants (and soon, an internet staple: cats) are being hidden under the banner of immoral content, but that’s the price of a censorship program devoid of nuance.

In discussing the problem with Indonesians, the advice we receive is to rely on such visitors already having circumvented these prohibitive measures, whether through a VPN, Tor, or similar browser extensions. This isn’t an altogether satisfactory solution, however, because we can’t expect everyone to be proactive—the onus falls on us to ensure that everyone has freedom of access, whatever platform is used.

So, what’s left is for either, a) Vimeo to stop being censored, or b) us not to use Vimeo to showcase videos. If the former doesn’t happen (and there is no evidence that it will, because Vimeo allows artistic nudity and sexual content), then there may be little choice on the next step forward.

Finally, we have to apologise for our absence over the past few months. There is no valid excuse for such tardiness, but alas, we have to provide one. Scenoptica is run by two people, a husband and wife team. As a result, any misstep in life invariably affects us both, and therefore the project.

Now, like all humans, we are not immune from tragedy, and a recent such event has been so destabilising, that things will quite literally never be the same. This will be in both good and bad ways, so we will take our lumps as they come, but we will also refrain from using them as an excuse for failing to live up to expectations. It’s taken some time to get back on our feet, and we appreciate the patience in the meantime. Sorry for being away for so long.

16 May 2015
